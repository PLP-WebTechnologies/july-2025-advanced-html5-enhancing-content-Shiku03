The Big Five

Originally coined by 19th-century big-game hunters to describe the most dangerous and challenging animals to hunt on foot, the term, Big Five , has since been embraced by the tourism industry as a symbol of Africa's rich biodiversity.

In East and Southern Africa, the term Big Five refers to five of the continent's most iconic and sought-after wildlife species, namely:

  • The African Lion
  • The African Elephant
  • The Cape Buffalo
  • The Leopard
  • The Rhinoceros:
    • Black Rhino
    • White Rhino

These animals are spread across different countries in the Eastern and Southern regions of Africa. The different countries are:

  1. Kenya
  2. Tanzania
  3. Uganda
  4. Rwanda
  5. Botswana
  6. Namibia
  7. Zambia
  8. Zimbabwe
  9. Mozambique
  10. South Africa

These animals can be found across renowned national parks and game reserves such as:

  • Kenya's Maasai Mara
  • Tanzania's Serengeti National Park
  • South Africa's Kruger National Park
  • Botswana's Chobe National Park
